The Low-Down on the "Low-Rider": A Corgi Grooming & Health Guide

Pembroke Welsh Corgis are a strong, athletic, and lively little herder who is affectionate and companionable without being needy, making them one of the most popular breeds in the United States.

Owning a Corgi (whether a Pembroke or a Cardigan) is like living with a sentient loaf of bread that has a very loud opinion about everything. While they are undeniably cute, their unique physiology requires a specific maintenance routine to keep them happy and healthy.

The Pembroke Welsh Corgi is a cattle herding dog breed that originated in Pembrokeshire, Wales. The name Corgi is of Welsh origin, and is a compound of the words cor and ci, meaning "dwarf" and "dog", respectively. It is one of two breeds known as a Welsh Corgi, the other being the Cardigan Welsh Corgi

  • Managing the Corgi Glitter (Shedding)

    Corgis are double-coated dogs, meaning they have a soft undercoat for insulation and a coarse outer coat for weather protection.

  • The Feet Phenomenon

    Because Corgis are so low to the ground, their paws act like mops. They pick up mud, burrs, and allergens with ease.

  • Protecting the Long Back

    The most critical health aspect of a Corgi is their spine. Due to their long bodies and short legs, they are prone to Intervertebral Disc Disease (IVDD).

  • The Judgmental Loaf: Mental Health

    Corgis are herding dogs, not lap dogs. A bored Corgi is a destructive Corgi.
